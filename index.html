Here are The big 5

A rhino is a large, herbivorous mammal characterized by its thick, protective skin and one or two horns on its snout. There are five species of rhinos: white rhinoceros, black rhinoceros, Indian rhinoceros, Javan rhinoceros, and Sumatran rhinoceros

An elephant is the largest land animal, characterized by its long trunk, large ears, and tusks (in some species). There are three species of elephants: the African bush elephant, the African forest elephant, and the Asian elephant.

Buffaloes, whether African or American bison, boast fascinating traits. They're remarkably resilient, adapted to diverse environments, from the grasslands of Africa to the prairies of North America.

Lions, often regarded as the kings of the animal kingdom, possess a range of intriguing characteristics. They are social felines, living in groups known as prides, typically consisting of related females, their offspring, and a few adult males.

Leopards are captivating big cats renowned for their elusive nature and adaptability. Their striking spotted fur provides exceptional camouflage in a variety of habitats, allowing them to blend seamlessly into their surroundings as they stalk prey or evade predators.
